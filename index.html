ChinaTown on Thayer

Chinatown on Thayer’s webpage was the subject of my redesign.

I am an avid fan of their food/services and believed the restaurant’s page deserved a makeover that reflected the top-tier quality of my own eating experience.

Issues with the Original Website

The original website is overall unorganized, and contains various issues including:

  • The top menu bar is very unorganized, and aligned in a non-linear manner.
  • The "Google Play" and "App Store" links are broken, and it's unclear why they are there in the first place. This is a page for a restaurant, not an app.
  • The contrast between the text and the dark blue background improves accessibility, but distracts form the overall color scheme.
  • The white text in the middle of the page raised flags for accessibility issues, due to very low contrast.

Low and High-Fidelity Prototypes

  • Improved color scheme consistency, using a white background for most of the project for easier reading.
  • Removed the confusing links to the Google Play Store and App Store, instead adding a button for Snackpass.
  • Removed text within images to avoid low contrast accessibility issues.
